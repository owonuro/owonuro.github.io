Anger

CONCEPT OF ANGER ANGER QUOTATIONS

“Anger is anger, holy or not” – Adeboye

“Speak when you are angry and you will make the best speech you will ever regret.” – Ambrose Bierce

“Anger is an acid that can do more harm to the vessel in which it is stored than to anything on which it is poured.” – Mark Twain

“Anger dwells only in the bosom of fools.” - Albert Einstein

“For every minute you remain angry, you give up sixty seconds of peace of mind” – Ralph Waldo Emerson

When angry, count one to ten before you speak. If very angry, count to one hundred.” – Thomas Jefferson

“Whatever is begun in anger ends in shame.” – Benjamin Franklin

“When anger rises, think of the consequences” - Confucius

“Anger is never without a reason, but seldom with a good one.” - Benjamin Franklin

“People won’t have time for you if you are always angry or complaining.” – Stephen Hawking

“Never respond to an angry person with a fiery comeback, even if he deserves it.” - The greatest remedy for anger is delay.

“Never be too angry beyond repairs. Anger is nothing good to be part of your attributes. Are you angry with someone? The sun is sinking, just drop it now” - Israelmore Ayivor

“When you are offended at any man’s fault, turn to yourself and study your own failings. Then you will forget your anger.” - Epictetus

“There are two things a person should never be angry at, what they can help, and what they cannot.” - Plato
